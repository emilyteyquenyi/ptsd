Follow the steps below to help one determine if they have PTSD

Happening of a
TRAUMATIC
event

PTSD commonly begins with a traumatic event that has happened more than a month. Some common events include accidents, natural disaster, sexual assaults etc. The person can be both the victim OR the witness of an event when it happens.

PTSD common
MISCON
-CEPTIONS

ASD (Acute Stress Disorder) shows common symptoms as PTSD, however isn't the same. It is common for individuals to experience ASD a month during the event of a traumatic event. However, an individual is diagnosed with PTSD if ASD continues after a month. It is important that close members of the patient determines the symptoms as early as possible before symptoms worsen.

How it
IMPACTS
one's life

The individual may never be able to overcome their fear after the incident. Therefore, they may not be able to do things as simply as a normal person would. This could affect their lives in various ways such as at their workplace, in social events or on a normal daily basis

Commonly associated with
VETERANS

PTSD is commonly associated with veterans due to events such as war and terrorism. Veterans have a higher chance of experiencing the lost of mateship in the battle field. This results in a higher chance of being diagnosed with PTSD after leaving the base camp after retirement.

Recognise the
SYMPTOMS

To be diagnosed with PTSD, individuals have to meet 2 symptoms each from all 4 criterias as stated across this slideshow.

    CRITERIA A

  • Unwanted Upsetting Memories
  • Nightmares
  • Flashbacks
  • Emotional Distress
  • Physical Reactivity

    CRITERIA B

  • Avoidance of trauma-related throughts/feelings
  • Avoidance of trauma-related
    external reminders

    CRITERIA C

  • Inability to remember
    parts of the event
  • Persistent negative beliefs
  • Blaming on self or others
  • Fear, Anger, Guilt, Shame
  • Lost of interest in activities
  • Social Detachment
  • Inability to experience
    positive emotions

    CRITERIA D

  • Irritability or agression
  • Risky, Destructive Behaviour
  • Hypervigilance
  • Increased Startled Reaction
  • Difficulty Concentrating
  • Difficulty sleeping

Your actions can
HELP

Providing support to close members of yours is effective when done correctly. Let them know you're there for them. Similar to depression, they need a sense of belonging in the family. An individual who is patient, thoughtful and selfless to accept his current situation. They need to be loved and feel loved.
